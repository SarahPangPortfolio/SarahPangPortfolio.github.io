Overview

Nov. 2022 - Dec. 2022 (5 Weeks)

YVR's Innovative Travel Solutions ( ITS) team is looking to expand the reach of indigenous artwork residing within the airport, specifically to individuals that are unable to visit the physical locations. This is part of the airport's reconciliation efforts with not only the Musqueam, their land being occupied by YVR, but also all the indigenous communities in British Columbia. Our team is given the opportunity to investigate the different possible mediums used to virtually house the indigenous artwork in YVR. Discovering examples of already existing digital museums that provide an immersive experience that guides the viewer through the space.

Investigating Existing Digital Museums

As the leader and point of contact of the group, I suggested investigating different types of remote digital museums and analyzing the unique interfaces of each. The thought process was to have users evaluate the interface of each and therefore suggest a starting point in the ITS team can use. By physically visiting the YVR airport and going on a tour of the artwork, I was able to have a deeper understanding of what the user flow would look like. With this knowledge, my group and I researched different digital museums which I further analyzed and categorized into interactive web gallery, interactive scrolling timeline, 360 virtual museum tour, and 3D space capture.

Study

The next step was to create the questionnaires that the participants would complete before, during, and after the interface evaluations. I specifically worked on separating the questions between interest in content versus interest in interface to differentiate the two answers. The team conducted a quantitative usability test which we used to obtain insights and feedback when observing individuals navigate through each interface. In addition to this test, the think-aloud method was used to understand the participant's behaviours, feelings, and thoughts as well as identify pain points while the participants are exploring the webpage.

The Study Results

After gathering participants and conducting the study, I helped in organizing and analyzing the answers to the questionnaires, specifically noting what the interface did well, what did poorly, and potential adjustments. What came out of my part of the analysis is how the 3D space capture website provided an immersive experience while allowing free movement. However, the interface struggled with displaying details regarding the content and making the navigation controls easy to understand.

Organization of Results

Proposed Digital Museum

I proposed and designed a mockup that includes onboarding, exploration, and information details pages. I concluded the project by writing a summary of the study and what the collected results indicated while adding suggestions to what the ITS team may do moving forward. This includes the use of legends for accessibility, indicators for navigation, details card for content, and an open-world concept for design.

Onboarding

Key Points:

  1. Instructions
  2. Language selection
  3. Legend

The results indicated that there needs to be an introduction to the interface in order for users to be able to browse the contenet at ease and solely focus on what is being displayed. The onboarding page also provides options in Canada's official languages thus provinding an option between English and French.

Exploration

Key Points:

  1. Interactive art
  2. Background music
  3. Navigation bar
  4. Search artists
  5. Customize experience

Moving on from the onboarding page, the user will be brought to the browsing location and can freely move within the enviornemnt, interacting with the art. The background music helps with the immersive experience. At the bottom left, icons from the onboarding page reside there as well as the settings cog on the right to help the user personalize their experience in terms of view sensitivity and audio volume. On the this page, the artwork in the museum would also be intractable through an indicator.

Artwork Details

Key Points:

  1. Title of artwork
  2. Artist name
  3. Audio readings
  4. Artwork information
  5. Gallery of pictures

When the artwork is selected by the user, an information details card pops up holding the title of the artwork, artist name, audio readings, artwork information, and a gallery of pictures. The content may vary but the visual language is to be the same in order for consistency and legibility issues.
