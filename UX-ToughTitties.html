Tough Titties Gallery

UX Design

Overview

April 16 - 24, 2023 (1 Week)

Tough Titties Designs was created and founded by Emma Hands as a brand specializing in creating light-hearted designed items that promote female empowerment and raise awareness for breast cancer. This project was completed for and during the SFU UX Hackathon Eunoia.

Investigating Problems

I interviewed the founder Emma Hands to understand her work process as well as the future plans for her business. Through this conversation, I was able to gather insights that provided design oppertunities which were further iterated on through group affinity mapping excercises. The team selected "building community connections to the audience" as the problem space for the project. In order to determine whether Hand's customer base would be willing to come together as a community, I did user research and went through all of Hand's Etsy reviews which did indicate so. With this in mind, our group brainstormed as to how the problem should be approached, thus I was able to come up with the "how might we" statement that is to be used for the project.

How might we help customers who support women empowerment better connect with the business by building a sense of community in a space that has a desire of belonging and interactions?

Ideation Phase

Using the "how might we" statement and the insights gathered from the interview and user research, our group conducted 10 minutes of brainstorming where each person wrote down as many solutions as they could on sticky notes and drawings. With the ideas, the team would analyze the suggestions and plot the solutions on a graph between desireability and interactivity. The process was conducted twice before taking the the most desired and interactive sitkcy notes were categorized and developed further. The final proposed solution is a combination of my solution of interactive gallery of stories and a groupmate's idea of customization of breast icons.

Solution

Our proposed design includes an extension of Hand's website whis has an interactive space where customers can connect with one another and share personal stories and experiences. The solution uses breast icons to represent individuals, and displays written stories when the icons are selected. In order to maintain the brand identity, our team aimed to maintain a fun and light-hearted approach, inspired by Hands' designs, despite the serious problem space. We adopted Tough Titties Designs' simple visual design and branding colors, and added customizable boob shapes to enhance their existing variety. With a mockup, my group and I conducted user testing using questionnaires, interviews, and think-aloud method. The feedback provided major insights on the mockup's pain points which were further iterated on in the solution.

Idea Categorization

User Testing

The Card

Key Points:

  1. Emphasis on calling for action
  2. Clearly stated purpose and goal

In order to bridge the physical and real-life customers onto one digital platform, the card will be sent or given to the customer after each purchase. Through user testing, we found that it was important to have a clear purpose of card, so we went through many iterations to ensure strong call to action with a direct layout. Being careful to not overload the card with information and leave some mystery and ambiguity to lead the customer to explore more.

Onboarding

Key Points:

  1. Telling the users what to expect
  2. Introduce the purpose of the site

Once the customer reaches the site either from Hand's main website or through the card, the user is greeted with an onboarding page. This page would not only serve as instructions on how to use the site, but also an introduction as to what the purpose is about. Providing an explorative context to the website and prepare the users for what to be expected. Through user testing, we found that users were unsure what their actions would do at the gallery, thus hints were included to provide reasonings behind the specific features.

Gallery

Key Points:

  1. Display of 10 random titties
  2. Filtering through search bar and filter button
  3. Shuffle the 10 titties display

After the onboarding page, the user would be brought to the gallery where 10 random pairs of titties would be displayed. This is meant to mimic the real life gathering experience, where one would never know who they will meet. By scrolling to the very end, a shuffle button is present to let users explore a new set of stories as the 10 pairs of titties are changed into new ones. A filtering and search feature was also added to help users quickly find the stories in which they resonate with. After reciving feedback from both Hands and users, a shop button was added to link the gallery with the products that Hands offers. The interaction on this page is hovering where the titty would not only enlarge and change color, but also display the name associated with the story to make the interaction more rewarding.
