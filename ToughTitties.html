Tough Titties Gallery

UX Design

Overview

April 16 - 24, 2023 (1 Week)

Tough Titties Designs was created and founded by Emma Hands as a brand specializing in creating light-hearted designed items that promote female empowerment and raise awareness for breast cancer. This project was completed for and during the SFU UX Hackathon Eunoia.

Investigating Problems

I interviewed the founder Emma Hands to understand her work process as well as the future plans for her business. I was able to gather insights from the interview and did group affinity mapping excercises to determine a problem space. By going through all of Hand's Etsy reviews, I noticed the need for a connecting space. Through further brainstorming, I was able to come up with the "how might we" statement that is to be used for the project.

How might we help customers who support women empowerment better connect with the business by building a sense of community in a space that has a desire of belonging and interactions?

Ideation Phase

Using the "how might we" statement, our group conducted brainstorming sessions to create a solution. With the ideas, the team would analyze the suggestions and plot the solutions on a graph between desireability and interactivity. The most desired and interactive sitkcy ideas were categorized and developed into a combination of my solution of interactive gallery of stories and a groupmate's idea of customization of breast icons.

Solution

Our proposed design is an extension of Hand's website whis has an interactive space where customers can connect with one another and share personal stories and experiences using titties. In order to maintain the brand identity, our team aimed to maintain a fun and light-hearted approach, inspired by Hands' designs, despite the serious problem space. With a mockup, my group and I conducted user testing using questionnaires, interviews, and think-aloud method in order to get feedback and insights which were further iterated on in the solution.

Tough Titties Brand

User Testing

The Card

Key Points:

  1. Emphasis on calling for action
  2. Clearly stated purpose and goal

In order to bridge the physical and real-life customers onto one digital platform, the card will be sent or given to the customer after each purchase. Through user testing, we found that it was important to have a clear purpose of card, so we went through many iterations to ensure strong call to action with a direct layout.

Onboarding

Key Points:

  1. Telling the users what to expect
  2. Introduce the purpose of the site

Once the customer reaches the site either from Hand's main website or through the card, the user is greeted with an onboarding page. This page would not only serve as instructions on how to use the site, but also an introduction as to what the purpose is about. Through user testing, we found that users were unsure what their actions would do at the gallery, thus hints were included to provide reasonings behind the specific features.

Gallery

Key Points:

  1. Display of 10 random titties
  2. Filtering through search bar and filter button
  3. Shuffle the 10 titties display

After the onboarding page, the user would be brought to the gallery where 10 random pairs of titties would be displayed. This is meant to mimic the real life gathering experience, where one would never know who they will meet. After reciving feedback from both Hands and users, a shop button was added to link the gallery with the products that Hands offers.

Create Your Story

Key Points:

  1. The progress bar at the top
  2. Code checking status
  3. Help link for user assistance

To create one's own story, the user can select "Your Story +" which would be directed to an access code page. The code from the physical card is to prevent users, who have not purchased items from Hands before, from creating a story as they would not be considered part of Hands' customer base. Addresses the heuristic of visibility of system status, a progress bar at the top helps users identify the process.

Customization

Key Points:

  1. “I don't feel represented” button
  2. Size adjustment and controls
  3. Ability to choose nothing for the customization
  4. Respects on privacies
  5. Emphasis on breast cancer survivors

Following the access code, the user will be brought to the customization page where user can express themselves through Hands' drawings and also contribute to the gallery with their own identity. The only required fields are the message itself as well as categorizing the message being related to breast cancer or not. This was added after the user testing when the issue of wanting to view stories that promoted over all women empowerment rather than breast cancer was brought up. Lastly, the confirmation states what is to happen next in order for the uwer's story to be part of the database and the user can return back to the gallery.

View Story

Key Points:

  1. Stamping to allow engagement
  2. Features improving the intimacy of the experience

When the viewer selects a pair of titties, the viewer would be brough to the story in which the user has created. Here, a stamping function is available for the viewer to either appreciate, support or admire the story through selecting the approriate sticker and placing it on the page. These functions on this page are to help create a community that has a positive enviornment and oppertunities to reach out.
