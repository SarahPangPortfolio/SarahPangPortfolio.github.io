Festival Jazzdor

UI Design

Overview

Feb. 2023 - April 2023 (6 Weeks)

The company "Jazzdor" holds a music festival in both Germany and France, where contemporary jazz performers and producers showcase their compositions and improvised music. The objective of this project is to utilize principles and qualities of other designers to create a poster which would then be translated into a microsite.

Design Precedents

By studying two designers, I was able to take out 3 design quailties from Massimo Vignelli and 2 principles from Ellen Luton. After being further reworeded by the team, the final design used one from each designer and are as follows:

Content Strategy

During the later weeks of the microsite interations, I was in charge of determining the content strategy and creating the appropriate copywriting for my groupmates to follow when desiining the interface. Here, the concenpt of after even archive was brought up and had the users be categorized into reliving experience and discovery.

Festival Lineup

Interactions:

  • Hover on vinyl to reveal sound sample
  • Click on vinyl to reveal artist details

The vinyl covers are animated in a belt-like fashion, providing users with an intuitive and seamless browsing experience, allowing them to easily select and click on their desired artist.

Artist Details

Interactions:

  • Click on boxes to expand
  • Click on close button to collapse elements
  • Click on next button to navigate to image gallery

Users will reveal parts of the microsite that contains the artist details. When the user clicks on the box, it expands to reveal the information that is initially hidden from the user.

Image Gallery

Interactions:

  • Click to stack new image

Users can view the image gallery by clicking on the next button. As the user clicks on an image, a new image will stack on top of it, which replicates the feeling of flipping through vinyl records in a stack. The image sequence will loop itself until the user clicks away from the gallery.

Performance Preview

This part of the microsite is to provide a preview of what the performance of the artist would be like.
